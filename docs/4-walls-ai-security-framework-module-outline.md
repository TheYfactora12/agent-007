# 4 Walls AI Security Framework — Module 1 Outline

## Module Title

AI Security Control Room: The 4 Walls Framework

## Target User

Credit union executives, security leaders, and risk owners who need a plain-language way to understand AI security without becoming deep technical specialists.

## Module Goal

Help leaders understand the four decision walls that determine whether AI use is safe, governed, useful, and teachable inside a regulated environment.

By the end of the module, the learner should be able to:

- explain the 4 Walls model in plain language
- identify where an AI initiative is weak or missing controls
- ask better governance questions before approval
- distinguish useful AI experimentation from risky AI theater

## Teaching Promise

This module should make AI security feel governable, not mystical.

## The 4 Walls

### Wall 1 — Purpose Wall

Question:

```text
Why are we using AI here at all?
```

Key teaching points:

- define the business problem first
- identify the target user
- state what better outcome is expected
- reject AI usage with no clear decision or value

Leader takeaway:

If the purpose is vague, the AI initiative is not ready.

### Wall 2 — Data Wall

Question:

```text
What data is the AI touching, and what must never cross the line?
```

Key teaching points:

- classify the data before tool selection
- separate public, internal, confidential, and regulated data
- define what cannot be sent to external tools
- require explicit boundaries for storage, retention, and reuse

Leader takeaway:

Most AI risk starts as a data-boundary failure before it becomes a model problem.

### Wall 3 — Control Wall

Question:

```text
What controls prove this use is safe enough to proceed?
```

Key teaching points:

- define approval thresholds
- require human review where risk is meaningful
- require logging, version awareness, and rollback paths
- match controls to the actual risk tier, not generic fear

Leader takeaway:

Good governance is not stopping work. It is making risk visible and controlled.

### Wall 4 — Learning Wall

Question:

```text
What did we learn, and how does the next team get smarter?
```

Key teaching points:

- capture what worked
- capture what failed
- preserve reusable prompts, checklists, and examples
- turn one experiment into a teachable asset

Leader takeaway:

AI maturity compounds only if learning is captured and reused.

## Recommended Module Flow

1. Open with a real leader problem: too much AI noise, not enough control.
2. Introduce the 4 Walls as a control-room model.
3. Walk through one example initiative against each wall.
4. Show where the initiative passes, fails, or needs controls.
5. End with one governance action the learner can use immediately.

## Example Scenario

Example:

```text
A credit union team wants to use a public AI assistant to summarize member complaint trends.
```

Use the walls to ask:

- Purpose: what decision improves?
- Data: does complaint content include regulated or sensitive information?
- Control: who approves the workflow and reviews outputs?
- Learning: what gets documented for the next team?
